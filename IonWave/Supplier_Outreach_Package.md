## Why This Matters

The Rollout Narrative calls supplier data "the most important data point in the entire rollout." Two fatal failure modes exist:

1. **CoA fails regulatory thresholds** (heavy metals above limits) = hard stop, cannot sell
2. **Landed COGS exceeds $25-30/box** = financial model may not close at $47-59 pricing

This data arrives AFTER execution begins unless we pre-validate NOW. No entity is needed to request quotes and CoA samples. This is the single highest-value de-risking action available.

## CoA Evaluation Template

When a supplier provides a Certificate of Analysis, evaluate against these thresholds:

### Heavy Metals (PASS/FAIL — any failure = hard stop)

| Contaminant | IonWave Maximum | FDA Reference | CA Prop 65 Limit | Action if FAIL |
|-------------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Lead (Pb) | < 0.5 ppm | No explicit limit | 0.5 ug/day | HARD STOP — do not proceed |
| Mercury (Hg) | < 0.1 ppm | 0.1 ppm | 0.3 ug/day | HARD STOP — do not proceed |
| Cadmium (Cd) | < 0.5 ppm | No explicit limit | 4.1 ug/day | HARD STOP — do not proceed |
| Arsenic (As) | < 10 ppb | 10 ppb | 10 ppb | HARD STOP — do not proceed |

**Why Prop 65 matters**: California is a major market. If product exceeds Prop 65 limits, it either requires warning labels (damages premium positioning) or can't be sold in CA. IonWave thresholds are set to the stricter of FDA and Prop 65.

### Microbiological (PASS/FAIL)

| Test | IonWave Maximum | Action if FAIL |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| Total Plate Count | < 1,000 CFU/g | FAIL — contamination risk |
| Yeast & Mold | < 100 CFU/g | FAIL — storage/processing issue |
| E. coli | Not detected | HARD STOP — safety issue |
| Salmonella | Not detected in 25g | HARD STOP — safety issue |

### Mineral Profile (VERIFY — not pass/fail but important)

| Mineral | Expected Range | Action if Outside |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Sodium | 900-1,100 mg per 10ml | Flag — may need reformulation |
| Potassium | 150-250 mg per 10ml | Flag — may need supplementation |
| Magnesium | 40-80 mg per 10ml | Flag — may need supplementation |
| Total minerals | 78+ elements detectable | Verify — supports "78 minerals" marketing claim |

### Optional (Differentiator, Not Blocking)

| Test | IonWave Target | Cost | Frequency |
|------|---------------|------|-----------|
| Microplastics | < 1 particle per 100ml | $2K-5K per test | Quarterly |
| Full 78-element panel | Consistent batch-to-batch | $300-500 per test | Every 5th batch |

When you have supplier quotes, plug into this matrix:

### Per-Box COGS Components

| Component | Target | Acceptable | Kill Zone |
|-----------|--------|------------|-----------|

| Electrolyte blend additives | $2.40-3.60 | $3.60-5.00 | > $5.00 |
| Flavoring + sweetener | $2.10-3.60 | $3.60-5.00 | > $5.00 |

| Box + insert | $1.20-2.10 | $2.10-3.00 | > $3.00 |
| Manufacturing/filling | $1.50-3.00 | $3.00-5.00 | > $5.00 |
| QA testing (amortized) | $0.60-1.20 | $1.20-2.00 | > $2.00 |
| **TOTAL PRODUCT COGS** | **$14.40-23.70** | **$23.70-35.00** | **> $35.00** |

### Landed COGS (Product + Logistics)

| Scenario | Product COGS | Fulfillment | Shipping | Processing | Returns | Total Variable | Gross Margin at $59 |
|----------|-------------|-------------|----------|------------|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Optimistic** | $17.40 | $2.50 | $3.50 | $2.01 | $4.13 | $29.54 | **50%** |
| **Realistic** | $20.00 | $4.00 | $4.25 | $2.01 | $4.13 | $34.39 | **42%** |
| **Conservative** | $25.00 | $6.00 | $5.00 | $2.01 | $4.13 | $42.14 | **29%** |

### Decision Gates

| Landed COGS/Box | Gross Margin at $59 | Decision |
|----------------|---------------------|----------|
| < $20 | > 66% | GREEN — proceed with confidence |
| $20-25 | 58-66% | GREEN — proceed, optimistic scenario |
| $25-30 | 49-58% | YELLOW — proceed with caution, realistic scenario |
| $30-35 | 41-49% | ORANGE — kill-or-pivot conversation. Consider price increase to $69 (matches Seaonic) |
| > $35 | < 41% | RED — Trade not viable at current pricing. Major strategic decision required. |

**Key reference**: Seaonic sells at $69/box with (presumably) similar COGS. If our COGS is $30-35, pricing at $69 instead of $59 yields 49-57% GM — potentially viable but changes all downstream hypotheses (CAC, conversion, churn).

## Red Flags (Disqualify Immediately If 3+)

1. Won't provide samples or charges excessively for them
2. Can't provide certifications or CoA
3. Requires 100% upfront payment (50/50 is standard)
4. Prices are suspiciously low (if it seems too good to be true)
5. Poor communication or very slow responses
6. No verified reviews or trade references
7. Won't provide customer references
8. Pressure to order immediately or "limited time" pricing

**1-2 red flags**: Proceed with caution, document concerns.
**3+ red flags**: Disqualify. Move to next supplier.

## Supplier Qualification Checklist (Pre-Contract)

Before signing any agreement, verify all of the following:

- [ ] FDA registration number verified in FDA database
- [ ] GMP certificate current (request copy, check expiry)
- [ ] CoA provided for recent batch (heavy metals, microbial, salinity — evaluated per template above)
- [ ] Product liability insurance >= $2M (request Certificate of Insurance)
- [ ] Production capacity confirmed for scale (can do 10K units/month?)
- [ ] Lead time verified in writing (not verbal)
- [ ] MOQ acceptable (<= $25K first order OR <= 5,000 units)
- [ ] Payment terms workable (Net 30 preferred; 50% deposit acceptable)
- [ ] Facility tour offered (virtual or in-person)
- [ ] Exact ocean sourcing location disclosed (region + depth at minimum)
- [ ] Processing method documented (cold micro-filtration preferred)
- [ ] Sample received and tested (taste, appearance, salinity)
- [ ] References provided (2+ US market customers)
- [ ] No exclusivity clauses blocking backup suppliers
- [ ] Price lock for 12 months or first 2 orders
- [ ] Reorder MOQ specified (ideally lower than initial)
- [ ] Termination clause fair (90-day notice, prorated refunds)

**Pass**: >= 14 of 17 items verified. **Fail**: < 14 — escalate concerns before signing.

## Ocean Sourcing Verification Questions

Ask during supplier evaluation. Answers support marketing claims and regulatory compliance.

**Location & Extraction:**
- Exact coordinates or region of extraction point?
- Depth of extraction?
- Extraction method (pump, pipeline, manual)?
- Frequency (continuous, seasonal, batch)?

**Processing:**
- Transportation from extraction to processing (food-grade tanks)?
- Storage conditions before processing (temperature, light, duration)?
- Processing method (cold micro-filtration? pore size?)?
- 0.22 micron filtration for sterile filtration?

**Quality:**
- Heavy metals CoA for last 3 batches?
- Microplastics testing (emerging standard, major differentiator)?
- Salinity verification (hypertonic 3.3% vs isotonic 0.9%)?
- Batch-to-batch mineral consistency (78-element panel)?

**Supply Chain:**
- Backup sourcing plan if primary location unavailable?
- Seasonal variability in availability or quality?
- Inventory on hand (buffer stock)?
